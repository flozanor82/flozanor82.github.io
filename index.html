Felipe A. Lozano-Rojas

I am a Ph.D.(c) in Public Affairs at Indiana University. My research interests span from applied microeconomics to social policies to their public finance consequences. I use the tools of data scence and causal inference to answer the reserach questions I set myself to answer. I hold concentrations in Public Finance and Policy Analysis.

My dissertation, Three Essays on the Unintended Consequences of Social Policy, explores how implementation and design of social policies result on differences with respect to the ideal outcomes expected from theory. My job market

During my time as a PhD I have been advised by advised by Maureen Pirog and Justin Ross.

I enjoy developing statistical and computational methods that help bbiologists understand complex, structured data. I have worked with David Relman’s group on the human microbiome and with Catherine Blish’s group on natural killer cell repertoires.
